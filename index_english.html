About Elvis

Elvis grew up in Pucallpa and was raised by his grandfather after his parents died in the civil war. His grandfather—an experienced shaman—began training him at the age of 16. Since he was 18, he has traveled extensively along the Amazon, gaining valuable jungle experience. For over 25 years, he has practiced Ayahuasca medicine in various villages between Pucallpa and Iquitos, leading rituals for locals and visitors—occasionally traveling abroad for this purpose. He now lives with his partner Mathilde and their children in the community... 100km upriver from Iquitos, and is committed to supporting the community.

Tours

Jungle Tours

Experience unforgettable jungle tours that can last many days and are flexibly tailored to your wishes. Elvis's partner Mathilde provides traditional and authentic meals. Tours of 6 or 7 days are especially popular to fully immerse yourself in the diverse flora and fauna of the Amazon.

Depending on the tour duration and included services, prices range from 90 to 110 US dollars per day.

Tour starting points: Pucallpa or Iquitos.

Important: The costs for travel to and from the lodge are not included in the price. However, Elvis knows the local providers and can organize affordable transport for you: From Iquitos, it’s about a two-hour shared taxi ride (approx. 4 US dollars) to Nauta, followed by a three-hour boat ride further into the wilderness (approx. 50 US dollars), with most of the cost going to fuel.

Ayahuasca Ceremonies

The number of Ayahuasca ceremonies can be freely chosen.

They always take place in the evening. The Ayahuasca brew is prepared together over the course of a day. Since a ceremony requires a preparatory diet of about three days—during which you should abstain from oil, sugar, salt, alcohol, caffeine, sex, and other drugs—this is taken into account during the cooking process. For example, the diet can begin on the second day, allowing a ceremony to take place on the 4th, 5th, and 6th evenings. Alternatively, the diet can be started before your arrival to plan ceremonies with a rest day in between or for a short stay.

During the day, there is time for jungle hikes, animal observation, learning about traditional plants—or simply resting in a hammock.

On a 6 or 7-day tour, 2 to 3 ceremonies can be easily integrated.

The cost is 110 US dollars per person per ceremony.

Kambo Ritual

Kambo is a traditional shamanic cleansing ritual using the secretion of a jungle frog (Phyllomedusa bicolor) living in the region. The substance is applied in tiny amounts to small, scratched areas of the skin. It is considered a powerful cleanse on a physical, energetic, and spiritual level.

The effect is intense and starts quickly. The goal is to release blockages and strengthen the immune system. The Kambo ritual is performed on request for 40 US dollars.

Accommodation

Enjoy authentic lodging right in the middle of the jungle—perfect for experiencing the nighttime magic of the Amazon. Each room has its own bathroom with a shower. Power is available for a few hours daily to charge devices and for cooking. The accommodations are all built slightly elevated, as water levels can rise significantly during the rainy season. The buildings are rain- and mosquito-proof and are only accessible by boat. If you wish, you can swim right in front of the lodge—and with a little patience, you might even spot dolphins directly from your doorstep. Traditional food is served. Sometimes, if you find and bring back macambo fruit kernels from a jungle hike, you can enjoy them roasted. Vegetarian meals are available upon request, and other special dietary needs can be accommodated within the local possibilities.

*Accommodations may vary depending on availability.

Ayahuasca

Ayahuasca is a traditional South American plant brew made from the vine Banisteriopsis caapi and the leaves of Chacruna (Psychotria viridis). It has been used for centuries by indigenous shamans to enter altered states of consciousness in communal ceremonies, experience spiritual healing, and gain deep insights into one's inner self.

The ceremony usually takes place at night, led by an experienced shaman. After a phase of attunement and purification, the Ayahuasca brew is offered in small sips. This is followed by a 4-6 hour journey, accompanied by traditional Ikaros (healing songs), where physical, emotional, and spiritual blockages can be released.

The Ayahuasca brew is cooked together, which takes a full day. Preparation starts early in the morning, before sunrise, with the collection of additional ingredients if needed.
